M.S. Dhoni

A legendary cricketer known for his calm leadership, sharp finishing skills, and transformative impact on Indian cricket.

Here's a timeline of MS Dhoni's life and career:

  • 1981 – Born on July 7 in Ranchi, Bihar (now Jharkhand), India.
  • 1999-2000 – Made his debut for Bihar in Ranji Trophy.
  • 2004 – Debuted for India in One Day Internationals (ODIs) against Bangladesh.
  • 2005 – Scored his maiden ODI century (148) against Pakistan in Visakhapatnam. – Made his Test debut against Sri Lanka.
  • 2007 – Captained India to victory in the inaugural ICC T20 World Cup.
  • 2008 – Became India's Test captain and led the team to the No. 1 Test ranking for the first time. – Received the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna award.
  • 2010 – Married Sakshi Singh Rawat.
  • 2011 – Captained India to victory in the ICC Cricket World Cup, hitting the winning six in the final. – Conferred with the honorary rank of Lieutenant Colonel by the Indian Territorial Army.
  • 2013 – Led India to victory in the ICC Champions Trophy, becoming the only captain to win all ICC white-ball tournaments.
  • 2015 – Dhoni and Sakshi welcomed their daughter, Ziva.
  • 2017 – Stepped down as limited-overs captain, passing the baton to Virat Kohli.
  • 2018 – Awarded the Padma Bhushan by the Government of India.
  • 2020 – Announced his retirement from international cricket on August 15.
  • 2023 – Continued to play for Chennai Super Kings (CSK) in the IPL, leading them to multiple titles.
  • Present – Dhoni remains a cricketing icon, actively contributing to CSK and pursuing interests beyond cricket.

This timeline highlights key moments in Dhoni's journey, capturing his achievements, personal milestones, and contributions to Indian cricket.
